Everything you need to know about preventive dental care

Preventive dental care focuses on maintaining oral health and preventing dental problems before they occur. It includes regular cleanings, dental exams, fluoride treatments, sealants, and oral hygiene education. The goal is to keep your teeth and gums healthy through proactive measures rather than waiting for problems to develop.

Most patients should visit for preventive care every six months for regular cleanings and exams. However, some patients may need more frequent visits based on their risk factors, such as those with gum disease, frequent cavities, or certain medical conditions. We'll recommend the appropriate schedule based on your individual needs.

Dental sealants are thin protective coatings applied to the chewing surfaces of back teeth to prevent cavities. They are most beneficial for children and teenagers, but adults can also benefit from them. Sealants protect the deep grooves and pits where food particles and bacteria can easily get trapped, reducing the risk of tooth decay.

Fluoride treatments are highly effective in preventing tooth decay and strengthening tooth enamel. Professional fluoride applications can reduce cavity risk by up to 40% in children and adults. The treatment remineralizes weakened enamel and makes teeth more resistant to acid attacks from bacteria and sugary foods.

Regular cleanings and exams cost significantly less than fillings, root canals, or other restorative procedures. By catching issues early and preventing problems, you can avoid expensive treatments and maintain better oral health.
