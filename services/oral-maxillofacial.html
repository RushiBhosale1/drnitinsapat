Everything you need to know about oral & maxillofacial surgery

Oral and maxillofacial surgery is a specialized branch of dentistry that focuses on surgical procedures involving the mouth, jaw, face, and neck. It includes wisdom tooth extraction, jaw surgery, facial trauma repair, dental implants, and treatment of oral pathology and facial deformities.

Wisdom tooth extraction is typically needed when teeth are impacted, causing pain, infection, or damage to adjacent teeth. Other reasons include overcrowding, difficulty cleaning, and potential orthodontic complications. Early removal often prevents future problems and complications.

Jaw surgery, or orthognathic surgery, corrects jaw misalignment and facial deformities. It's needed for severe bite problems, breathing difficulties, speech issues, and facial asymmetry. The procedure improves both function and appearance, often combined with orthodontic treatment for optimal results.

Recovery time varies depending on the procedure. Simple extractions may take 3-7 days, while complex jaw surgery can take 6-8 weeks for full recovery. We provide detailed post-operative care instructions and follow-up appointments to ensure optimal healing and monitor your progress.
